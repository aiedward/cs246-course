Lesson 06 Overview

This week you will be exposed to multithreaded programming, that is, having your program run multiple tasks "simultaneously."

Your weekly team activity will give you experience with the basics of starting and stopping threads, and then your individual assignment will build on these concepts and apply them to an Android GUI.

In addition to getting started with Android, you should be making progress on your team projects. This week you will be doing the research or proof of concept work you identified last week to help mitigate the biggest risks in your project.

Lesson Objectives:

  1. Understand the principles of multithreaded programming
  2. Research team project risks

Individual Assignment

You are responsible to read about design patterns and take the quiz by Wednesday evening.

Also, there is an individual assignment due on Saturday night on multithreaded programming.

Please note that this is the last real individual programming assignment. From this point on, you will be devoting your time to working on your team project. In that regard, beginning next week, you will be expected to work at least 10 hours per week on this course. This will help make sure your projects stay on track and that each team member is making a solid contribution every week.

Team Project

This week as a team, you need to identify the pieces of your project that are unknown to you or carry the largest risk. Some examples may include, looking into any APIs you will need to use, tracking down any algorithms that you need (e.g., processing sound or image data), or looking into file formats that you need to handle.
